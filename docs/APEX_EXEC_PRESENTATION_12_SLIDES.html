APEX Energy Trading & Risk Management

The Databricks pitch: a single Lakehouse operating model for three very different power markets.
NEM EPEX ERCOT React + FastAPI Delta + Unity Catalog MLflow + Notebooks Databricks Apps

Market Scope Explained

  • NEM (Australia): five-minute dispatch market with FCAS and unit-level operational constraints.
  • EPEX (Europe): auction-centric day-ahead and intraday structure with cross-border coupling dynamics.
  • ERCOT (Texas): nodal real-time LMP market with ancillary products and scarcity-driven volatility.

Why One Databricks Platform

  • Delta + SQL Warehouses create one analytics engine despite different market microstructures.
  • Unity Catalog enforces common definitions for price, exposure, VaR, and portfolio value.
  • Workflows + Apps convert ingestion, forecasting, and user actions into one operating loop.
Executive message: APEX is not three disconnected regional tools; it is one Databricks-native control plane adapted to NEM, EPEX, and ERCOT specifics.

Set the Scene: Business Pressure

Each market has different cadence, products, and risk profile, but leadership still needs one auditable decision system.

How the 3 Markets Differ in Practice

  • NEM: dispatch operators react every few minutes to unit availability, constraints, and FCAS opportunities.
  • EPEX: traders optimize day-ahead positions, then rebalance intraday as weather and demand shift.
  • ERCOT: desks manage nodal spread risk, congestion, and rapid scarcity pricing events.

Why Databricks Matters for This Mix

  • One Lakehouse architecture standardizes ingest, feature engineering, analytics, and app serving.
  • Serverless SQL exposes common KPIs while preserving market-specific business logic.
  • Unity Catalog lineage gives executives traceability from source feeds to user decisions.

Cross-Market Activities APEX Supports

  • Ingest market prices, forecasts, and telemetry continuously.
  • Run dispatch, trade, and risk workflows from shared data contracts.
  • Execute portfolio planning with explainable scenario assumptions.

Databricks Pitch on Slide 2

  • Without Databricks: three stacks, duplicate governance, inconsistent numbers.
  • With Databricks: one governed platform with reusable jobs, models, and app patterns.
  • Result: faster rollout of new markets with lower operational risk.
