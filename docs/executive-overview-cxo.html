The Business Case: From Reactive to Prescriptive

The Hidden Cost of Traditional Maintenance

Traditional maintenance strategies leave significant value on the table:

Maintenance Approach Industry Benchmark Hidden Costs
Reactive (Run to Failure) Still used by 30-40% of operations 5-10x higher repair costs, cascade failures, safety incidents, reputation damage
Preventive (Time-based) Standard practice for most industries 30-50% over-maintenance, unnecessary parts replacement, production disruption
Predictive (Condition-based) Adopted by leading manufacturers Better but lacks financial context—operators still don't know when or if intervention is economically justified
Prescriptive (ROI-driven) Next-generation approach Optimal intervention timing with quantified business impact—maximizes uptime while minimizing total cost

What Makes This Prescriptive, Not Just Predictive?

Most predictive maintenance tools answer: "What asset will fail?"

This platform answers: "What should we do, when should we act, and what is the financial impact?"

  • Expected failure cost vs. planned intervention cost — Quantify the business case for every maintenance decision
  • Optimal intervention windows — Balance production schedules, parts availability, and crew capacity
  • Avoided cost tracking — Demonstrate maintenance ROI to finance and board leadership
  • Work order prioritization — Focus resources on highest-impact activities, not just highest risk
